EDGE Data Project – Part 2: Analysis

This section explores how EDGE’s incentive programs shape economic development across Memphis. Using five datasets provided by EDGE, I analyzed investment patterns, job creation, and geographic distribution to understand how these programs support businesses and communities.

Guiding Questions

The analysis answers three core questions:

  • How much investment has EDGE supported across Memphis, and how does it vary by program?
  • How much did each program contribute to total jobs, which program contributed the most?
  • How is EDGE investment distributed across Memphis ZIP codes or neighborhoods?

1. Investment Across Programs

Guided Question 1: How much investment has EDGE supported across Memphis, and how does it vary by program?

  • Across all programs, EDGE has supported approximately $2.04 billion in total capital investment across Memphis.
  • Most of this investment comes from tax-incentivized programs—PILOTs, Bonds, and TIFs—while direct financial support programs, such as loans and grants, represent a much smaller share.
  • This pattern reflects the design and purpose of EDGE’s tools. Tax-incentivized programs do not require direct public spending, making them lower-risk and more scalable for large capital projects.
  • These programs also align with EDGE’s mission to stimulate long-term economic activity, support job creation, and encourage place-based development that benefits surrounding communities—not just individual firms.
